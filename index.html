About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at the University of the Bundeswehr Munich and the TUM. My research started with image retrieval for geo-localization, but has since shifted to more general approaches to sampling in deep learning. I am particularly interested in contrastive learning and the dynamics of sampling, and how they affect learning efficiency, generalization, and convergence speed. I also investigate how insights from multimodal learning and image synthesis can contribute to these challenges.

If you want to collaborate just write me a mail.

Research Interests

  • Computer Vision: image retrieval, cross-view image matching, image synthesis
  • Multimodal Deep Learning: multimodal representations, large multimodal models
  • Machine Learning: contrastive learning, representation learning, hard negative sampling
